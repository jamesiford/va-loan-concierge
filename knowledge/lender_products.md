## 1. VA Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL)

### Product Overview
The IRRRL allows Veterans with an existing VA loan to refinance to a lower rate with
minimal documentation. Valor Home Lending offers two IRRRL tiers:

- **IRRRL Standard** — full-service with lender credit options
- **IRRRL Express** — streamlined processing, closes in as few as 21 days

> Rates shown are for well-qualified borrowers. Your actual rate depends on credit score,

### IRRRL Lender Overlays (Valor-Specific Requirements)
Valor Home Lending imposes the following overlays above VA minimum guidelines:

| Requirement | VA Minimum | Valor Overlay |
|---|---|---|
| Credit score | No minimum | **620** minimum FICO |
| Maximum LTV | 100% (no appraisal) | 100% (appraisal waived if no cash out) |
| Maximum DTI | No hard limit | **60%** back-end DTI |
| Seasoning | 210 days / 6 payments | **6 months** from first payment |
| Delinquency | Not in default | No 30-day lates in past 12 months |
| Loan amount | No limit | Up to **$2,000,000** |

### IRRRL Eligible Costs (Can Be Rolled Into Loan)
- VA Funding Fee (0.50% — waived if exempt)
- Origination fee (max 1% of loan amount)
- Title and title insurance fees
- VA appraisal fee (if required by lender)
- Recording fees and transfer taxes
- Prepaid interest (up to 60 days)
- Hazard insurance premium (1 year)

### IRRRL Ineligible Costs (Cannot Be Rolled In)
- Discount points (beyond 2 points)
- Real estate commissions
- Prepayment penalties
- Repairs or renovations

## 2. VA Cash-Out Refinance

### Product Overview
The VA Cash-Out Refinance allows Veterans to refinance any existing mortgage (VA,
conventional, FHA, or USDA) into a new VA loan while taking cash out of their home equity.

Unlike the IRRRL, a Cash-Out Refi **requires full underwriting**:
- Full appraisal required
- Income and employment verification required
- Full credit underwriting required
- COE required

### Cash-Out Eligibility
- Veteran must currently **occupy the property** as primary residence
- Property must have sufficient equity (see LTV limits below)
- Veteran must have a valid COE
- Must meet all VA and lender eligibility requirements

### Cash-Out LTV Limits

| Property Type | Maximum LTV |
|---|---|
| Single-family (1 unit) | **100%** of appraised value |
| 2–4 unit (owner-occupied) | 100% |
| Manufactured home | 100% |
| Condos (VA-approved) | 100% |

## 3. VA Jumbo Loan

### Product Overview
A VA Jumbo loan is any VA purchase or refinance loan that exceeds the conforming loan
limit for the county where the property is located. Despite the "jumbo" label, VA Jumbo
loans still have no required down payment for Veterans with full entitlement.

### How VA Jumbo Works (With Full Entitlement)
Veterans with full entitlement can borrow above conforming limits with **no down payment**
as long as they qualify under lender guidelines. The VA guarantees 25% of the loan,
and Valor Home Lending holds the remaining risk.

### VA Jumbo With Remaining Entitlement
If the Veteran has remaining (partial) entitlement, a **down payment may be required**:
```
Required Down Payment = (Loan Amount – Available Entitlement × 4) × 25%
```

### VA Jumbo Loan Limits (Valor Home Lending)

| Tier | Loan Amount | Down Payment (Full Entitlement) |
|---|---|---|
| Standard VA | Up to county limit | 0% |
| VA Jumbo I | $806,501 – $1,500,000 | 0% |
| VA Jumbo II | $1,500,001 – $2,000,000 | 0% |
| VA Jumbo III | $2,000,001 – $3,000,000 | 10% (lender requirement) |

### VA Jumbo Overlays (Valor-Specific)

| Requirement | Jumbo I & II | Jumbo III |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum FICO | **680** | **720** |
| Maximum DTI | 50% | 45% |
| Reserves required | 6 months PITI | 12 months PITI |

## 4. VA Renovation Loan

### Product Overview
The VA Renovation Loan (also called VA Rehab or VA Reno) allows Veterans to finance
the purchase **and** renovation of a home in a single loan. This product is based on the
**after-improved value** of the property, not the as-is value.

### Two Product Variants

#### VA Renovation Standard
- Renovation costs: up to **$150,000**
- Requires a licensed general contractor
- Requires detailed scope of work and bids at application
- Draw schedule: up to 5 disbursements
- Renovation period: up to **6 months** from closing

#### VA Renovation Limited (Minor Repairs)
- Renovation costs: up to **$35,000**
- No general contractor required for work under $15,000 per trade
- Simplified documentation
- All work must be completed within **3 months** of closing

### Eligible Renovation Work
- Structural repairs and additions
- Kitchen and bathroom remodels
- Roof, HVAC, electrical, and plumbing replacement
- Accessibility modifications (ramps, widened doorways)
- Energy efficiency improvements (solar, insulation, windows)
- Landscaping and site work (up to 15% of total renovation cost)
- New appliances permanently affixed to the property

### Ineligible Renovation Work
- Luxury items (swimming pools, hot tubs, outdoor kitchens)
- Detached structures that are not primary dwelling
- Work already completed before loan closing
- Furniture, artwork, or personal property

### VA Renovation Overlays (Valor-Specific)

| Requirement | Valor Overlay |
|---|---|
| Minimum FICO | **660** |
| Maximum LTV (after-improved) | **100%** |
| Maximum renovation amount | $150,000 (Standard) / $35,000 (Limited) |
| Contractor approval | Valor-approved contractor list required |
| Contingency reserve | 10% of renovation cost required |
| Maximum DTI | 55% |

### VA Renovation Rates (March 2026 — illustrative)
Rate is set at closing based on the **standard VA 30-year fixed rate** plus a renovation
premium:

## 5. Lender Credit and Pricing Options

Valor Home Lending offers flexible pricing structures:

### Lender Credit Options
Borrowers may choose to take a **lender credit** in exchange for a higher interest rate,
reducing out-of-pocket closing costs. This is common for Veterans who want minimal
upfront expense.

| Credit Amount | Rate Increase (approx.) |
|---|---|
| $1,000 credit | +0.125% to rate |
| $2,500 credit | +0.250% to rate |
| $5,000 credit | +0.500% to rate |

### Discount Points
Borrowers may pay **discount points** to buy down the interest rate. One point = 1% of
the loan amount.

| Points Paid | Rate Reduction (approx.) |
|---|---|
| 0.5 points | –0.125% |
| 1.0 point | –0.250% |
| 2.0 points | –0.500% |

**Note**: The VA limits origination fees to **1% of the loan amount**. Discount points
are separate from the origination fee and are not subject to the 1% cap, but must be
bona fide (i.e., they must result in a rate reduction proportional to cost).
