# VA Loan Entitlement — Calculations, Restoration & Simultaneous Use

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 3 | VA Pamphlet 26-7 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA entitlement rules at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. What Is Entitlement?

VA entitlement is the dollar amount the VA guarantees on a Veteran's behalf. Lenders
require this guarantee before approving a VA loan. Entitlement is not the loan limit —
it is the VA's maximum liability if the borrower defaults.

There are two entitlement tiers:

| Tier | Amount | Also Called |
|---|---|---|
| Basic entitlement | $36,000 | "Tier 1" |
| Bonus (secondary) entitlement | Up to $91,600 in most counties | "Tier 2" / "Second-tier" |
| **Combined** | **$127,600 in most counties** | "Full entitlement" |

Full entitlement in high-cost counties is higher — it equals 25% of the conforming loan
limit set annually by FHFA. In 2025, the conforming limit for most counties is $806,500,
making full entitlement $201,625. In the highest-cost counties (e.g., San Francisco), the
conforming limit is $1,209,750, making full entitlement $302,438.

---

## 2. How the Guaranty Works

The VA guarantees **25% of the loan amount** up to the Veteran's available entitlement.
Most lenders require a full 25% VA guaranty to approve the loan with no down payment.

**Example — full entitlement, no down payment:**
- Loan amount: $500,000
- Required VA guaranty: $125,000 (25%)
- Available entitlement: $201,625 (2025 standard)
- Entitlement covers the 25% requirement → **$0 down payment required**

**Example — reduced entitlement:**
- Veteran has $36,000 of entitlement remaining (prior VA loan still active)
- Loan amount: $300,000
- Required guaranty: $75,000 (25%)
- Available entitlement: $36,000
- Gap: $75,000 − $36,000 = $39,000
- Down payment required: $39,000 (Veteran must cover the gap in cash)

---

## 3. Remaining Entitlement Formula

When a Veteran has an active VA loan, remaining entitlement is calculated as:

```
Remaining Entitlement = Full Entitlement − Entitlement Used on Active Loan
```

The entitlement used on the active loan equals **25% of the original loan amount**.

**Example:**
- Veteran's active VA loan balance (original): $320,000
- Entitlement used: $320,000 × 25% = $80,000
- Full entitlement (2025): $201,625
- Remaining entitlement: $201,625 − $80,000 = $121,625
- New loan limit with no down payment: $121,625 × 4 = $486,500

## 4. Full Entitlement — When It Applies

A Veteran has **full entitlement** (no cap) when they:

1. Have never used the VA home loan benefit, OR
2. Previously used it and paid the loan off AND restored entitlement via VA Form 26-1880, OR
3. Previously used it, paid it off, and the one-time restoration of entitlement has been
   applied (automatic on first payoff after prior use)

Since the Blue Water Navy Vietnam Veterans Act (2020), Veterans with full entitlement have
**no effective loan limit** — they can borrow as much as a lender will approve with no
down payment requirement, subject to county conforming limits for guaranty calculation.

---

## 5. Entitlement Restoration

### One-Time Automatic Restoration
A Veteran who has paid off a prior VA loan and sold the property (or transferred the
property by assumption with the assuming borrower taking on the VA obligation) may
request one-time automatic restoration. This is processed via:
- **VA Form 26-1880** — Request for Certificate of Eligibility
- Or via ACE (Automated Certificate of Eligibility) if the lender is a SAR-approved
  lender and the payoff history is visible in VA systems

### Full Restoration Conditions
Full entitlement restoration is available when:
- The prior VA loan has been **paid in full**, AND
- The Veteran **no longer owns the property** (sold or transferred), OR
- The prior VA loan was assumed by another Veteran who substituted their own entitlement

### One-Time Restoration Without Selling
A Veteran may receive **one-time** restoration even while still owning the property
(keeping it as a rental, for example) if the loan is fully paid off. This one-time
restoration is a lifetime benefit — it can only be used once without selling.

## 6. Simultaneous Use of Entitlement (Two Active VA Loans)

A Veteran may have **two active VA loans at the same time** as long as:
- The Veteran has sufficient remaining entitlement to cover 25% of the new loan, OR
- The Veteran makes a down payment to cover the entitlement gap

**Common scenario — PCS (Permanent Change of Station):**
A servicemember with an active VA loan on their current home is relocated. Rather than
selling, they retain the first home as a rental and purchase a new primary residence using
remaining entitlement. Both loans are simultaneously guaranteed by the VA.

**Lender checklist for simultaneous use:**
1. Pull current COE — verify entitlement used on first loan
2. Calculate remaining entitlement
3. Determine if remaining entitlement covers 25% of the new loan amount
4. If not, calculate required down payment
5. Document rental income on the first property per VA underwriting guidelines

---

## 7. Surviving Spouse Entitlement

An unremarried surviving spouse of a Veteran who died in service or from a
service-connected disability is eligible to use the VA home loan benefit as if they
were the Veteran. The surviving spouse's COE will show entitlement in their name. Key
rules:
- The surviving spouse's entitlement is the same as a Veteran's full entitlement
- If the deceased Veteran had an active VA loan, the surviving spouse does **not** inherit
  the used entitlement — they receive fresh entitlement
- Surviving spouses who remarry after age 57 (and before December 16, 2003) may retain
  eligibility under certain conditions — lenders should contact the VA RLC for guidance

## 8. Reading the Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

The COE displays entitlement in one of three ways:

| COE Display | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `$36,000` | Basic entitlement only — first-time buyer or reduced entitlement scenario |
| `$0` | All entitlement is in use — cannot use VA benefit without restoration or down payment |
| `$XX,XXX` | Specific remaining amount — partial entitlement available |
| Blank / "Eligible — Not Determined" | ACE system could not determine; manual COE request required |

Since 2020, COEs for Veterans with full entitlement may display the message **"This
Veteran's basic entitlement is $36,000. Additional entitlement is also available."**
This is the standard display for Veterans with full entitlement — it does not mean they
are limited to $36,000.
