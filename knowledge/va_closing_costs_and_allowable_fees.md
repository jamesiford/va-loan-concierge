# VA Loan Closing Costs — Allowable Fees, Prohibited Charges & Seller Concessions

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 8 | VA Circular 26-23-15 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA fee regulations at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

VA loan closing costs are governed by the VA's fee limitations, which protect Veterans
from excessive charges. The VA distinguishes between fees the Veteran **may** pay,
fees the **lender** must absorb, and fees that are **prohibited** entirely. Understanding
these rules is essential for loan officers, real estate agents, and Veterans.

---

## 2. The 1% Origination Fee Rule

The VA limits lender origination charges to **1% of the loan amount** (flat fee). This
covers all of the lender's overhead for originating the loan. Alternatively, lenders may
use the "itemized" approach — charging less than 1% if the itemized actual costs are
lower.

**What the 1% covers (lender may not charge these separately):**
- Loan processing fee
- Underwriting fee
- Commitment fee
- Document preparation fee
- Tax service fee
- Automated underwriting system (AUS) fee
- Any other origination-related administrative charge

**What the 1% does NOT cover (charged separately, allowed):**
- VA funding fee
- Appraisal fee
- Credit report fee
- Title search and title insurance
- Recording fees
- Hazard / homeowners insurance premium
- Discount points (see section 6)

---

## 3. Allowable Fees — The "Reasonable and Customary" Standard

Beyond the origination fee, the Veteran may pay the following if they are **reasonable
and customary** for the market:

| Fee | Notes |
|---|---|
| VA appraisal fee | Set by the VA fee schedule for the county; varies by property type |
| Credit report | Must be actual cost; typically $30–$75 |
| Title examination | Reasonable for the market; lender may not mark up |
| Title insurance (lender's policy) | Standard lender policy |
| Recording fees | Actual government recording charges |
| State/local transfer taxes | If customary in the jurisdiction |
| Flood zone determination | One-time; typically $15–$25 |
| Survey (if required by title) | Reasonable market rate |
| Homeowners insurance (1 year prepaid) | Required at closing |
| Mortgage interest (per diem) | From closing date to end of first month |
| Initial escrow impounds | 2–3 months of taxes and insurance |
| Pest (termite) inspection | In applicable states |
| Home inspection | Paid outside closing; not a closing cost per VA rules |

---

## 4. Prohibited Fees — The "Non-Allowable" List

The following fees **may not be charged to the Veteran borrower**:

| Prohibited Fee | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Attorney fees for the lender | Lender's own legal costs are covered by the 1% origination fee |
| Mortgage broker fee | In addition to the 1% origination charge |
| Settlement/closing fee above VA limits | The settlement agent's charge must be reasonable |
| Document preparation fee | Covered by origination fee |
| Loan application fee | Covered by origination fee |
| Yield spread premium (YSP) | May not be separately disclosed as a borrower cost |
| Prepayment penalty | VA loans may never have a prepayment penalty |
| Rate lock extension fee | If the lock expires due to lender error or VA processing |
| Postage/courier fees charged to borrower | Lender overhead; not a Veteran cost |

**Who pays non-allowable fees?** These costs must be paid by the **seller**, the
**lender**, or the **real estate agent** — not the Veteran borrower.

---

## 5. Seller Concessions

The VA allows sellers to pay up to **4% of the established reasonable value (appraised
value or purchase price, whichever is less)** in concessions. This 4% limit applies to
"seller concessions" beyond standard seller-paid closing costs.

### What Counts Toward the 4% Cap

- Paying the Veteran's VA funding fee
- Paying down the Veteran's debt to improve DTI (payoff of car loan, credit card, etc.)
- Buydown of the interest rate (discount points)
- Prepaid expenses the seller agrees to cover (insurance, taxes, HOA dues)
- Gifts of personal property (appliances, furniture)

### What Does NOT Count Toward the 4% Cap

- Standard seller-paid closing costs (title insurance, escrow fees paid by seller per
  local custom)
- Real estate agent commissions
- VA appraisal fee (if seller agrees to pay it)
- Any cost that is customarily a seller expense in the local market

---

## 6. Discount Points

The Veteran borrower may pay discount points to buy down the interest rate. There is no
VA-imposed limit on discount points, but:

- Each point = 1% of the loan amount
- Points paid by the borrower are deductible interest for federal tax purposes (consult
  a tax advisor)
- The lender must clearly disclose points on the Loan Estimate and Closing Disclosure
- Points paid by the seller count toward the 4% concession cap

## 7. Cash-to-Close Calculation

For a VA purchase loan with no down payment:

```
Cash to Close = Prepaid Items + Escrow Impounds + Non-financed Closing Costs
             − Seller Concessions − Lender Credits
```

Because the funding fee can be financed, and because no down payment is required, many
VA borrowers close with **very low or zero cash out of pocket** — sometimes receiving
a credit at closing if seller concessions and lender credits exceed closing costs.

**Example:**
- Loan amount: $400,000 (funding fee financed = $408,600)
- Allowable closing costs: $6,200
- Prepaids and escrow: $4,800
- Total borrower costs: $11,000
- Seller concession (2%): $8,000
- Lender credit: $2,500
- Net cash to close: $11,000 − $8,000 − $2,500 = **$500**

## 8. IRRRL Closing Cost Rules

For VA IRRRL (streamline refinance) transactions, different rules apply:

- **No cash out of pocket required**: All closing costs may be rolled into the new loan
- **Recoupment test**: All fees (including 0.50% funding fee) must be recouped within
  **36 months** via the monthly payment reduction
- **Allowable to finance**: Origination fee (max 1%), appraisal (if ordered), title,
  recording, funding fee
- **Not allowed**: Cash back to the borrower; any fees not directly related to the IRRRL

### IRRRL Net Tangible Benefit Calculation

```
Monthly Savings = Old P&I − New P&I
Break-even Months = Total Financed Costs ÷ Monthly Savings
Pass Condition: Break-even ≤ 36 months
```

If the break-even exceeds 36 months, the IRRRL does not satisfy the VA's net tangible
benefit (NTB) test and the loan may not be approved as an IRRRL.

## 9. Good Faith Estimate / Loan Estimate and CD Requirements

VA loans are subject to standard RESPA/TRID disclosure requirements:

- **Loan Estimate (LE)**: Must be issued within 3 business days of application
- **Closing Disclosure (CD)**: Must be issued at least 3 business days before consummation
- **Tolerance rules**: Lender-controlled fees (origination, appraisal, credit report) are
  subject to zero-tolerance or 10% tolerance buckets per TRID
- **VA-specific**: The VA Loan Summary Sheet (VA Form 26-0286) must accompany the CD
  and must reflect the funding fee, entitlement used, and guaranty amount
