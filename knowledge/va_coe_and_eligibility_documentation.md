# VA Certificate of Eligibility (COE) — Issuance, Documentation & Common Issues

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 2 | VA Form 26-1880 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current COE procedures at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

The Certificate of Eligibility (COE) is the VA's official document confirming that a
Veteran, Servicemember, or surviving spouse meets the service requirements to use the
VA home loan benefit. Lenders must obtain a COE before approving a VA loan. The COE
also indicates:

- The amount of entitlement available
- Whether the borrower is exempt from the VA funding fee
- The borrower's prior loan history visible to the VA system

---

## 2. Four Methods to Obtain a COE

### Method 1: ACE (Automated Certificate of Eligibility)
- **Who uses it**: VA-approved lenders with SAR (Supervised Automatic Underwriter) status
- **How it works**: The lender submits the borrower's data through WebLGY; the system
  automatically issues the COE in seconds if VA records are complete
- **Success rate**: ~70% of applications are resolved instantly via ACE
- **When it fails**: Military service records not yet in the VA system (recent discharge),
  discrepancies in name/SSN/dates, National Guard members whose records are held by
  the state adjutant general

### Method 2: WebLGY Portal (VA.gov)
- Veterans may apply directly at VA.gov using their DS Logon or ID.me account
- Processing time: instant if records are digitized, 3–5 business days if manual review
  is needed
- The COE can be downloaded as a PDF and provided to the lender

### Method 3: eBenefits Portal
- Veterans may apply at eBenefits.va.gov
- Being phased out in favor of VA.gov — Veterans should be directed to VA.gov

### Method 4: Mail Application (VA Form 26-1880)
- **Use when**: Electronic methods fail or the Veteran's records are not in the VA system
- **Form**: VA Form 26-1880 — Request for a Certificate of Eligibility
- **Processing time**: 4–6 weeks (varies by VA Regional Loan Center volume)
- **Submit to**: The VA Regional Loan Center (RLC) with jurisdiction over the state where
  the Veteran lives

## 3. Required Documentation by Service Category

### Active Duty Servicemembers
- **Statement of Service** signed by the adjutant, personnel officer, or commanding
  officer, stating:
  - Full name
  - Social Security Number
  - Date of birth
  - Entry date on active duty
  - Duration of any lost time
  - Name of command providing the information

### Veterans (Discharged)
- **DD Form 214** — Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty
  - Must show character of service (honorable, general, etc.)
  - Copy 4 (Member Copy) is preferred; Member Copy 2 is also acceptable
  - If the Veteran does not have their DD-214, they may request it from the National
    Archives (NPRC) via Standard Form 180

### National Guard Members
- **If activated under Title 10 federal orders**:
  - DD Form 214 (issued upon release from federal active duty)
- **If never federally activated** (Title 32 service only):
  - NGB Form 22 (Report of Separation and Record of Service) from the National Guard
    Bureau
  - NGB Form 23 (Retirement Points Accounting) showing 6+ years of qualifying service
  - Statement of service with character of service

### Reserve Members
- DD Form 214 if activated to active duty, OR
- Statement of service showing 6+ qualifying years plus honorable discharge or current
  service

### Surviving Spouses
- Veteran's DD Form 214
- Marriage certificate
- Veteran's death certificate OR VA letter confirming the Veteran died in service or
  from a service-connected disability
- For surviving spouses receiving DIC: VA award letter (confirms exemption from funding fee)
- If the Veteran's death was not service-connected: surviving spouse is NOT eligible
  unless the Veteran had 90+ days of wartime service

## 4. Character of Discharge Requirements

| Discharge Type | VA Loan Eligibility |
|---|---|
| Honorable | Always eligible (if service requirements met) |
| General (Under Honorable Conditions) | Always eligible |
| Other Than Honorable (OTH) | Case-by-case — VA Character of Discharge review required |
| Bad Conduct Discharge (BCD) from special court-martial | Potentially eligible — VA review required |
| Dishonorable Discharge | Not eligible |
| Bad Conduct Discharge from general court-martial | Not eligible |

### Discharge Upgrade Process
Veterans with OTH or BCD discharges may apply for a discharge upgrade through:
- **Discharge Review Board (DRB)**: Applications within 15 years of discharge; handles
  upgrades to OTH and BCD from special courts-martial
- **Board for Correction of Military Records (BCMR)**: Handles all branches; no
  time limit; can address any discharge type
- A pending discharge upgrade does **not** make the Veteran eligible — the upgraded
  discharge must be officially recorded before COE issuance

## 5. Reading the COE

Key fields on the COE:

| Field | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Entitlement Code** | 1–11 code indicating service category and period; used for internal VA processing |
| **Entitlement Amount** | Dollar amount of entitlement available (see `va_entitlement_calculations.md`) |
| **Funding Fee Exemption** | "EXEMPT" if the Veteran has a service-connected disability |
| **Subsequent Use Indicator** | Blank = first use; "SUBSEQUENT USE" = prior use, higher funding fee applies |
| **Conditions** | Any special conditions attached to the COE (e.g., "IRRRL ONLY") |

---

## 6. COE Validity and Expiration

- A COE does **not expire** — once issued, it remains valid until used
- However, the entitlement information reflected on the COE is a point-in-time snapshot;
  if the Veteran's status changes (e.g., a prior VA loan is paid off and entitlement
  restored), a new COE should be obtained to reflect the current entitlement
- Lenders should pull a fresh COE at loan application, even if the Veteran has an older
  COE on file

## 7. Common COE Issues and Resolutions

### "IRRRL Only" Condition
- **Cause**: The Veteran's current entitlement is fully used on an active VA loan
- **Meaning**: The COE can only be used for an IRRRL — no new purchase or cash-out
  without entitlement restoration or a down payment
- **Resolution**: Confirm the active loan details; calculate remaining entitlement;
  advise Veteran on down payment requirement if they want a second VA loan for purchase

### Funding Fee Exemption Not Showing
- **Cause**: VA disability rating is pending or was recently awarded and not yet in
  the VA loan system
- **Resolution**: Contact the VA RLC with a copy of the Veteran's VA award letter;
  the RLC can update the COE manually. Do not close the loan with the fee if the
  Veteran has a documented rating — process the manual update first

### Name/SSN Mismatch
- **Cause**: Name change (marriage/divorce) or data entry error in VA records
- **Resolution**: Submit VA Form 26-1880 with supporting documentation (legal name
  change order, marriage certificate) for manual COE issuance

### Recent Discharge — Records Not Yet Available
- **Cause**: DoD records take 60–90 days post-discharge to reach the VA system
- **Resolution**: The lender may use the Statement of Service method in lieu of ACE
  for recently discharged Veterans

## 8. Entitlement Restoration — Documentation

To restore entitlement after paying off a prior VA loan:
- Submit **VA Form 26-1880** to the RLC, with:
  - Evidence the prior loan is paid in full (payoff statement or HUD-1/CD from sale)
  - Evidence the Veteran no longer owns the property (recorded deed of sale) — or
    for one-time restoration without sale, payoff confirmation only
- Processing time: 2–4 weeks via mail; faster via lender's WebLGY submission if the
  payoff is visible in VA systems
