# VA Loan State-Specific Overlays, Lender Overlays & Underwriting Guidelines

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 4 | VA Circular 26-24-09 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current overlay and underwriting requirements with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

VA loan underwriting has two layers: **VA guidelines** (federal minimum standards) and
**lender overlays** (additional requirements imposed by the individual lender). Borrowers
who do not qualify at one lender due to an overlay may qualify at another lender that
does not impose that restriction, even though both use VA backing.

Additionally, some states have laws that affect VA loan processing, closing procedures,
recording requirements, or attorney involvement.

---

## 2. VA Minimum Credit Standards (Federal Floor)

The VA does not publish a minimum credit score requirement. However, the VA does require:

- Satisfactory credit history for the past 12 months
- No more than one 30-day late payment on any revolving or installment debt in the
  previous 12 months
- Bankruptcy: Chapter 7 requires a **2-year seasoning** from discharge; Chapter 13
  requires **12 months of satisfactory payment** under the plan plus trustee approval
- Foreclosure: **2-year seasoning** from date of transfer of title
- Short sale: Generally treated the same as foreclosure — 2-year seasoning unless the
  Veteran was current at time of sale and there was no deficiency judgment

## 3. Common Lender Credit Overlays

Most lenders impose minimum credit score requirements above the VA federal floor:

| Lender Tier | Typical Minimum FICO | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Credit union / community bank | 580–620 | Often more flexible for their members |
| Non-bank VA specialist | 580–620 | Some lenders specialize in below-620 VA loans |
| Mid-tier retail lender | 620–640 | Most common retail threshold |
| Large bank VA lending | 640–680 | Major banks often have higher overlays |
| VA Jumbo ($1M+) | 720–740 | Near-universal overlay for high-balance loans |

**Borrower tip**: A Veteran with a 600 FICO score may be declined by a large bank but
approved by a VA specialist lender at the same terms. VA loans are assumable, so
origination flexibility matters.

## 4. Debt-to-Income (DTI) and Residual Income

### VA DTI Guideline
The VA uses **41% back-end DTI** as a guideline, not a hard cap. Loans above 41% DTI
may still be approved if the file has compensating factors, including:
- Strong residual income (significantly above the VA threshold)
- Substantial liquid assets (12+ months PITI in reserves)
- Excellent credit history (no late payments in 24 months)
- Long-term stable employment in the same field

### Residual Income — The VA's Primary Underwriting Test
The VA's residual income test is unique to VA lending. It measures the net monthly
income remaining after all obligations are paid, compared to a regional threshold based
on family size and property region.

**2025 Residual Income Thresholds (sample — Northeast region):**

| Family Size | Loan ≥ $80,000 | Loan < $80,000 |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | $450 | $390 |
| 2 | $755 | $654 |
| 3 | $909 | $788 |
| 4 | $1,025 | $888 |
| 5 | $1,062 | $921 |
| Each add'l member | +$80 | +$80 |

Veterans whose residual income exceeds the threshold by 20%+ are considered strong files
that can offset a high DTI.

**Calculation:**
```
Gross Monthly Income
− Federal, state, local income taxes (estimated)
− PITI (principal, interest, taxes, insurance including HOA)
− All other monthly debt obligations (car, student loans, credit cards min payment)
− Job-related expenses (childcare, union dues)
= Residual Income
```

## 5. Employment and Income Documentation

### W-2 Employees
- Most recent 2 years' W-2s
- Most recent 30 days' pay stubs
- Written VOE (Verification of Employment) for verbal confirmation of current employment
- 2-year employment history required (gaps over 30 days must be explained)

### Self-Employed Borrowers (25%+ ownership in a business)
- 2 years' personal federal tax returns (all schedules)
- 2 years' business tax returns
- Year-to-date P&L statement (if more than 3 months since last return)
- Income is the **2-year average** of net income plus allowable add-backs
- Income is declining year-over-year: VA requires using the lower year's income

### Military Income
- LES (Leave and Earnings Statement) — most recent
- VA disability compensation: award letter; no tax return required; grossed up to 125%
  of non-taxable amount
- BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing): fully countable; non-taxable; gross-up applies
- BAS (Basic Allowance for Subsistence): fully countable; non-taxable; gross-up applies

### Rental Income
- Property already owned: 2 years' Schedule E + current lease agreement
- Property being vacated to purchase new primary: 75% of market rent (verified by
  appraiser) may be counted if the borrower has a signed lease AND sufficient equity
  (25%+) in the departing residence

## 6. State-Specific Considerations

### Attorney State vs. Title Company State
- **Attorney states** (e.g., NY, NJ, GA, SC, MA): A licensed attorney must conduct or
  supervise the closing. Legal fees are a closing cost. VA allows this fee if it is
  reasonable and customary.
- **Title company states** (e.g., TX, CA, FL): A title company or escrow agent handles
  closing. No attorney required.
- **Hybrid states**: Some states permit either; local custom dictates practice.

### Texas VA Loans
- Texas has unique community property and homestead laws that affect VA cash-out
  refinances
- Texas Section 50(a)(6) rules limit cash-out refinances to 80% LTV (stricter than
  standard VA 100% LTV cash-out)
- VA IRRRL in Texas is generally not affected by 50(a)(6)
- Both spouses must sign the deed of trust even if only one is on the loan

### Community Property States
- AZ, CA, ID, LA, NM, NV, TX, WA, WI are community property states
- Non-borrowing spouse's debts must be reviewed for DTI impact even if they are not on
  the loan
- The non-borrowing spouse's credit does not affect VA loan approval but their debts
  count in the DTI calculation

### Transfer Taxes and Recording Fees
- Some states/counties impose significant transfer taxes that can affect cash-to-close:
  - New York City: up to 1.425% of purchase price
  - Pennsylvania: 2% (split buyer/seller)
  - Maryland: 0.5% state + county transfer tax
  - Most VA-heavy states (VA, NC, TX, FL): minimal or no transfer taxes

## 7. VA Loan Assumption Guidelines

VA loans are assumable by any creditworthy borrower — the assuming borrower does **not**
need to be a Veteran. Key rules:

- The VA must approve the assumption; the assuming borrower must qualify under VA
  underwriting standards
- If assumed by a non-Veteran: the original Veteran's entitlement remains tied to the
  loan and is not released until the loan is paid off
- If assumed by a qualifying Veteran who substitutes their own entitlement: the original
  Veteran's entitlement is restored

**Practical implication for IRRRLs in rising rate environments:**
A Veteran with a 3% VA loan (originated 2021) may find significant demand for assumption
rather than traditional sale. The assumption process requires VA approval (typically
45–60 days) and coordination between seller's lender, buyer, and the VA RLC.

## 8. Occupancy Requirements

VA loans require the Veteran to certify they intend to **personally occupy** the property
as their primary residence within a reasonable time (typically 60 days of closing). This
is an owner-occupancy loan program — not for investment properties.

Exceptions:
- **Active duty**: If the Veteran is deployed, a spouse or dependent may satisfy the
  occupancy requirement
- **OCONUS deployment**: If the Veteran is assigned overseas, the occupancy requirement
  may be deferred until return; lender must document the exception
- **IRRRL**: Occupancy requirement is less strict — the Veteran must certify they
  previously occupied the property; current occupancy is not required
- **Renting after purchase**: A Veteran who purchases with a VA loan and later rents
  the property (e.g., PCS relocation) does not violate the occupancy certification as
  long as the original intent was genuine
