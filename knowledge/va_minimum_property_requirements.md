# VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs)

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 12 | VA Pamphlet 26-7 Rev. Ch. 12 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA MPR standards at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs) are standards that a property must meet before a
VA loan can be approved. The VA appraiser — not a home inspector — is responsible for
identifying MPR deficiencies during the appraisal inspection. MPRs are designed to ensure
the property is **safe, sound, and sanitary** for the Veteran borrower.

MPRs are distinct from market value — a property may have full market value but still
fail MPR standards if it presents health or safety risks.

---

## 2. Core MPR Categories

### 2.1 Structural Integrity
- Foundation must be sound with no evidence of settling, heaving, or moisture intrusion
- Roof must have a remaining economic life of at least **2 years** and not permit moisture
  entry; if remaining life is questionable, a roofer's certification is required
- Walls, floors, and ceilings must be structurally sound with no significant damage
- Crawl spaces must be accessible, adequately ventilated, and free of standing water

### 2.2 Mechanical Systems
- **Heating**: Must be adequate for the climate; permanently installed (space heaters or
  wood-burning stoves are not acceptable as the primary heat source unless they can
  adequately heat the home)
- **Electrical**: Service panel must be adequate, no exposed wiring, no double-tapped
  breakers (unless breaker manufacturer explicitly permits), GFCI outlets required near
  water sources
- **Plumbing**: Must supply safe drinking water; hot and cold running water required;
  sewer or septic system must be functional and sanitary; well water requires testing

### 2.3 Water Supply
- Properties served by a private well require a water quality test
- Test must show potable water meeting local health authority standards
- Shared wells require a shared well agreement with recorded easements
- Distance requirements: well must be at least 50 feet from a septic tank drain field
  (100 feet in some jurisdictions)

### 2.4 Septic Systems
- Must be functioning properly with no evidence of surfacing sewage or failure
- If the system is near the end of its useful life or shows signs of failure, a septic
  inspection (pumping and inspection) is required
- VA requires adequate space for a replacement drain field on the property

### 2.5 Lead-Based Paint
- Properties built **before 1978** require inspection for deteriorated lead-based paint
  (chipping, peeling, scaling)
- Any deteriorated lead-based paint visible on the interior or exterior of the home must
  be remediated before closing
- Stabilization (encapsulation) is acceptable in most cases; full removal is required for
  surfaces accessible to children under age 6

### 2.6 Wood-Destroying Insects (Termites)
- Termite inspection required in **most states** (the VA publishes an annual list of
  exempt states where termite activity is rare)
- VA Form 26-1852 (Description of Materials) or a standard NPMA-33 report is required
- Active infestation or unrepaired damage must be remediated before closing
- The cost to treat and repair can be paid by any party (buyer, seller, or lender)

### 2.7 Access and Egress
- Property must have safe pedestrian access from a public or private street
- Private road access requires a recorded easement ensuring permanent access
- Each bedroom must have a window or door that provides emergency egress
- No bedrooms may be accessible only through another bedroom (no "tandem" bedrooms)

## 3. MPR vs. Market Value

A VA appraiser simultaneously establishes the **Notice of Value (NOV)** and identifies
any MPR deficiencies. These are separate determinations:

- An appraiser may determine the home is worth $450,000 AND flag a required roof repair
- The lender cannot close the loan until the MPR deficiency is corrected or escrowed
- The NOV is not reduced simply because MPR repairs are required (unless the deficiency
  affects market value)

---

## 4. Repair vs. Escrow

### When Repairs Must Be Completed Before Closing
- Any deficiency that presents an **immediate health or safety risk** must be repaired
  prior to closing — no escrow permitted
- Examples: active termite infestation, no functional heating in a cold climate, exposed
  electrical wiring, non-potable water supply

### When Repairs May Be Escrowed
- Non-critical cosmetic or structural issues with no immediate safety impact may be
  escrowed in some cases at lender and VA discretion
- Escrow amount must be 1.5× the estimated repair cost
- Repairs must be completed within a specified timeframe (typically 60–90 days after
  closing) and verified by re-inspection

---

## 5. New Construction vs. Existing Construction

### New Construction
- Properties must have a **1-year builder's warranty** (or a 10-year insured protection
  plan in lieu of the warranty)
- All systems must be installed and operational at the time of the appraisal inspection
- VA has the right to inspect at any stage of construction (VA Form 26-1859)
- If the builder uses a VA fee panel inspector, a VA fee appraiser performs a final
  inspection only

### Existing Construction
- Standard MPR inspection applies
- Age of systems is considered; an HVAC system near end of useful life may require
  evaluation even if still operational

---

## 6. Condominiums and Planned Unit Developments (PUDs)

- The condo project must be on the **VA-approved condo list** (searchable at VA.gov)
- Individual unit MPRs still apply
- Common areas and shared mechanical systems are reviewed at the project approval level,
  not the individual unit appraisal

---

## 7. MPR vs. FHA MPS (Minimum Property Standards)

| Dimension | VA MPRs | FHA MPS |
|---|---|---|
| Standard title | Minimum Property Requirements | Minimum Property Standards |
| Governing chapter | VA Lender's Handbook Ch. 12 | HUD Handbook 4000.1 |
| Appraiser scope | Appraiser identifies MPR issues during appraisal | Same — FHA appraiser identifies MPS issues |
| Lead paint | Required for pre-1978 properties | Required for pre-1978 properties |
| Termites | Required in most states | Not federally required (varies by lender) |
| Escrow for repairs | Limited — safety issues must be resolved pre-closing | More flexible — 203(k) program available |
| Condo approval | VA condo approval required | FHA condo approval required (separate list) |

## 8. Common MPR Issues That Delay VA Closings

Based on VA Regional Loan Center data, the most frequently cited MPR deficiencies are:

1. **Roof condition** — cracked, missing, or worn shingles; inadequate drainage
2. **Peeling/chipping paint** on pre-1978 homes — exterior trim and soffits
3. **Active wood-destroying insect** infestation or unrepaired damage
4. **Non-functional HVAC** or inadequate heating capacity
5. **Electrical hazards** — exposed wiring, double-tapped breakers, outdated panels
6. **Crawl space moisture** — standing water or inadequate ventilation
7. **Septic concerns** — signs of surfacing effluent or an aged system in a known-failure area

Lenders and Realtors working in VA purchase markets should proactively identify these
issues before the appraisal to avoid closing delays.
