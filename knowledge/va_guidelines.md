# VA Loan Guidelines — Eligibility, COE, IRRRL, Funding Fees, Entitlement & MPRs

> **Source**: VA Loan Concierge Knowledge Base | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA guidelines at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

## 1. Basic Service Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for a VA home loan, a Veteran, Servicemember, or surviving spouse must meet
one of the following service requirements:

### Active Duty
- **90 continuous days** of active duty during wartime, OR
- **181 continuous days** of active duty during peacetime

### National Guard / Reserves
- **6 years** of service in the Selected Reserve or National Guard, OR
- **90 days** of active duty under Title 10 orders (including at least 30 consecutive days)

### Discharge Requirements
Discharge must be under conditions other than dishonorable. Eligible discharge types:
- Honorable
- General (Under Honorable Conditions)
- Other Than Honorable — may be eligible on a case-by-case basis; requires VA review

### Surviving Spouses
An unremarried surviving spouse of a Veteran who died in service or from a
service-connected disability is eligible. Spouses of MIA/POW Veterans may also qualify.

## 2. Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

A Certificate of Eligibility (COE) is required to confirm the borrower's entitlement to
VA loan benefits. It is issued by the Department of Veterans Affairs.

### How to Obtain a COE
1. **Through the lender**: Most VA-approved lenders can pull COEs electronically via
   the VA's Web LGY system in minutes.
2. **Online**: Veterans can apply at VA.gov using their DS Logon, My HealtheVet, or
   Login.gov credentials.
3. **By mail**: VA Form 26-1880 (Veterans) or VA Form 26-1817 (surviving spouses),
   mailed to the VA Eligibility Center.

### COE Entitlement Codes
The COE includes an entitlement code that indicates the basis for eligibility:

| Code | Basis |
|------|-------|
| 1 | WWII Veteran |
| 5 | Regular military (active duty) |
| 10 | Surviving spouse |
| 11 | Selected Reserve / National Guard |
| 13 | Active duty Servicemember |
| 31 | Purple Heart recipient |

### Entitlement Amount Shown on COE
- **Basic entitlement**: $36,000 (legacy figure; guarantees loans up to $144,000)
- **Bonus entitlement**: Calculated per county conforming loan limits
- Most Veterans have **full entitlement** if they have never used a VA loan or have
  paid off a prior VA loan and had entitlement restored

## 3. Entitlement & Loan Limits

### Full Entitlement
Veterans with full entitlement (COE shows $0 previously used or "exempt") have **no VA
loan limit** — they can borrow as much as a lender will approve with no down payment
required, subject to lender and ability-to-repay standards.

### Remaining Entitlement (Partial Use)
If a Veteran has an existing VA loan that has not been paid off, their remaining
entitlement determines how much they can borrow with no down payment on a second
simultaneous VA loan.

**Formula**:
```
Maximum Guarantee = (County Loan Limit × 25%) – Used Entitlement
No-down-payment loan = Maximum Guarantee × 4
```

**Example** (Maricopa County, AZ — 2026 loan limit: $806,500):
- Used entitlement: $100,000
- Maximum guarantee: ($806,500 × 25%) – $100,000 = $201,625 – $100,000 = $101,625
- No-down-payment loan amount: $101,625 × 4 = $406,500

### Entitlement Restoration
Entitlement is restored when:
- The VA loan is paid in full AND the property is sold, OR
- A qualified Veteran **assumes** the existing VA loan, OR
- A one-time restoration is granted without selling the property (if the prior loan is
  paid in full), OR
- The property is conveyed to the VA after foreclosure

## 4. VA Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL)

The IRRRL (also called the VA Streamline Refinance) allows eligible Veterans to refinance
an existing VA loan to a lower interest rate with reduced documentation requirements.

### IRRRL Eligibility Requirements
1. The loan being refinanced **must be a VA loan** — cannot refinance a conventional,
   FHA, or USDA loan into an IRRRL
2. The Veteran **must currently occupy or have previously occupied** the property as
   their primary residence (prior occupancy is sufficient — renters are eligible)
3. The new loan **must result in a lower interest rate** than the existing VA loan,
   **unless refinancing from an ARM to a fixed-rate loan** (rate increase permitted in
   this case only)
4. The new loan term must not exceed the original term by more than **10 years**
5. No cash may be taken out at closing (closing costs may be rolled in)

### IRRRL Streamline Features
- **No appraisal required** in most cases (lender may waive)
- **No income verification required** in most cases
- **No credit underwriting required** in most cases (lender overlays may apply)
- Certificate of Eligibility is **not required** — lender can confirm prior VA loan
  through the VA's database
- Occupancy certification: Veteran certifies **prior** occupancy (not current)

### IRRRL Net Tangible Benefit Test
As of February 2024, VA regulation 38 CFR Part 36 requires the IRRRL to provide a
**net tangible benefit** to the Veteran:

| Refinance Type | Required Benefit |
|---|---|
| Fixed → Fixed | New rate must be at least **0.50%** lower than old rate |
| ARM → Fixed | New rate may be higher — conversion benefit is sufficient |
| Fixed → ARM | New rate must be at least **2.00%** lower — rarely done |

### IRRRL Break-Even Requirement
The VA requires lenders to calculate the **recoupment period** — the number of months
for the Veteran to recover closing costs through lower payments. The recoupment period
**must not exceed 36 months** for the loan to be approved.

**Formula**:
```
Recoupment (months) = Total Closing Costs ÷ Monthly Payment Reduction
```

### IRRRL Funding Fee
| Veteran Type | Funding Fee |
|---|---|
| First use or subsequent use | **0.50%** of loan amount |
| Surviving spouse | 0.50% |
| Exempt (service-connected disability ≥ 10%) | **$0** — waived entirely |

## 5. VA Funding Fee Tables

The VA Funding Fee is a one-time fee paid at closing (or rolled into the loan) that
funds the VA home loan program.

### Purchase Loans — Funding Fee by Down Payment

| Loan Type | Down Payment | First Use | Subsequent Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Purchase | 0% | **2.15%** | **3.30%** |
| Purchase | 5%–9.99% | 1.50% | 1.50% |
| Purchase | 10%+ | 1.25% | 1.25% |
| IRRRL (any) | N/A | 0.50% | 0.50% |
| Cash-Out Refinance | N/A | 2.15% | 3.30% |

### Funding Fee Exemptions
The following borrowers are **exempt** from the VA Funding Fee:
- Veterans receiving VA compensation for a service-connected disability of **any rating**
- Veterans who would be entitled to compensation but are receiving military retirement pay
- Surviving spouses of Veterans who died in service or from service-connected disability
- Active duty Servicemembers who have received a Purple Heart

**Note**: Lenders must verify exemption status on the VA's CAIVRS / eBenefits system
before waiving the fee. Exemption is not self-certified.

## 6. Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs)

VA Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs) ensure that VA-financed homes are safe, sound,
and sanitary. The VA appraiser assesses MPR compliance during the appraisal.

### Core MPR Categories

#### Safety
- Property must be free of hazards that could affect occupants' health or safety
- No exposed lead-based paint on interior surfaces
- Adequate roof covering with expected life of **2+ years** remaining
- No active pest infestation (termite inspection required in most states)
- Functioning electrical, plumbing, heating, and cooling systems

#### Soundness
- Foundation must be structurally sound with no evidence of settlement or movement
- Crawl spaces must be accessible, properly vented, and free of moisture
- No evidence of active water intrusion in basement or crawl space
- Mechanical systems must be in working order and adequate for the property

#### Sanitation
- Adequate and continuous supply of potable water (well water requires testing)
- Functioning sewage disposal (septic systems must pass inspection)
- Water heater must meet local building codes and have a pressure relief valve
- Kitchen and bathrooms must be functional

### MPR Repair Process
If the appraiser notes MPR deficiencies:
1. Seller may agree to make repairs before closing
2. Veteran may make repairs (not standard; requires VA approval)
3. Lender may escrow funds for post-closing repairs (limited circumstances)
4. Transaction may proceed with a VA-approved Escrow Holdback if the repair is minor
   and not a safety issue

### MPR Waivers
Certain MPR requirements may be waived if:
- The property is being sold "as-is" and the Veteran acknowledges the condition
- The deficiency does not affect safety, soundness, or sanitation
- Waiver is requested by the lender and approved by the VA Regional Loan Center

## 7. Occupancy Requirements

### Primary Residence Requirement
VA loans are for **owner-occupied primary residences only**. The Veteran (or eligible
surviving spouse) must certify intent to personally occupy the property as their
primary residence within **60 days** of closing.

### Exceptions to 60-Day Requirement
| Situation | Extended Occupancy Allowed |
|---|---|
| Active duty / PCS orders | Up to **12 months** after closing |
| Deployed borrower | Spouse or dependent may satisfy occupancy |
| Major renovation required | Case-by-case; must occupy within 1 year |

### Multi-Unit Properties
Veterans may purchase a 2–4 unit property with a VA loan if:
- The Veteran occupies one unit as their primary residence
- Rental income from other units may be used to qualify (subject to lender guidelines)
