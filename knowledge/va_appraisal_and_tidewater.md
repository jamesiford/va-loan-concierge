# VA Appraisal Process — NOV, Tidewater Initiative & Reconsideration of Value

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 11 | VA Circular 26-20-14 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA appraisal procedures at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

The VA appraisal process is mandatory for all VA-guaranteed purchase and cash-out
refinance loans. It serves two purposes simultaneously:

1. **Establish the Notice of Value (NOV)** — the appraiser's determination of the
   property's market value
2. **Identify MPR deficiencies** — confirm the property meets VA Minimum Property
   Requirements (see `va_minimum_property_requirements.md`)

The VA appraisal is ordered through the VA's appraisal management system (WebLGY) by the
lender — the borrower or agent may not order it directly. The appraiser is assigned
from a VA fee panel roster for the county where the property is located.

---

## 2. Notice of Value (NOV)

The NOV is the official VA appraisal result. Key facts:

- The NOV establishes the **maximum loan amount** the VA will guarantee (the loan may not
  exceed the lesser of the NOV or the purchase price)
- The NOV is **not a guarantee of value** — it reflects the appraiser's estimate of
  market value based on comparable sales (comps)
- The NOV is valid for **180 days** from the date of the appraisal report
- The NOV is **portable** — the borrower owns it and may use it with any VA-approved
  lender during the 180-day validity period; the lender who ordered it cannot withhold it

### What Happens When NOV < Purchase Price

If the appraised value comes in below the contract price, the borrower has three options:

1. **Renegotiate** — ask the seller to reduce the price to the NOV
2. **Pay the gap in cash** — bring the difference between NOV and contract price to
   closing as a cash contribution (the VA loan is limited to the NOV amount)
3. **Walk away** — VA purchase contracts must include a VA escape clause (VA Form
   26-6705c) that allows the buyer to exit without penalty if the NOV is below contract
   price

---

## 3. Tidewater Initiative

The Tidewater Initiative is a procedural step that allows the appraiser to request
comparable sales data from the parties **before finalizing the appraisal** when the
property may not appraise at the contract price.

### How Tidewater Works

1. The appraiser completes fieldwork and determines there may be insufficient comps to
   support the contract price
2. The appraiser notifies the VA appraisal system (WebLGY) — this triggers a **48-hour
   Tidewater period**
3. The lender, buyer's agent, and seller's agent are notified and may submit **additional
   comparable sales data** (not opinions of value — only verifiable sales data)
4. The appraiser reviews the submitted comps and completes the appraisal
5. If the submitted comps support the contract price, the appraisal may come in at value
6. If they do not, the final NOV will reflect what the market data supports

### Tidewater Is Not a Guarantee

Triggering Tidewater does not mean the appraisal will come in at value — it only ensures
the appraiser has reviewed all available data. Many Tidewater requests still result in
low appraisals.

### Buyer/Agent Best Practices for Tidewater

- Prepare a comp package **before** the appraisal is ordered, anticipating Tidewater
- Include: recent sales within 1 mile and 90 days (or expanded search if market is thin),
  active listings showing current demand, pending sales if available
- Submit within the 48-hour window — late submissions are not accepted
- Comps must have closed (settled) — pending sales are acceptable as supplemental data
  only

## 4. Reconsideration of Value (ROV)

If the NOV comes in below the contract price and the borrower believes the value is
incorrect, they may request a Reconsideration of Value (ROV) through the lender.

### 2024 ROV Rule Changes

VA Circular 26-24-XX updated ROV procedures effective May 2024:

- The borrower now has a **direct right to request an ROV** — previously, only the lender
  could initiate
- The borrower may submit alternative comps or point to specific errors in the appraisal
- The VA requires appraisers to provide written explanations when they reject submitted
  comps
- ROV requests must be submitted within **90 days** of the NOV date
- A second appraisal (fee review) may be ordered by the VA if the ROV raises substantive
  issues the first appraiser cannot resolve

### What Counts as Grounds for ROV

Valid grounds:
- Comparable sales that closed after the appraisal effective date and are more proximate
- Factual errors in the appraisal (wrong square footage, wrong bedroom count)
- Comps with demonstrably superior adjustments than those the appraiser applied

Invalid grounds:
- Disagreement with the appraiser's professional judgment without supporting data
- The contract price itself (the appraiser is not required to appraise at contract price)
- Comps in a significantly different neighborhood or market area

## 5. VA Appraisal Independence Requirements

The VA has strict independence rules for the appraisal process:

- The appraiser is **assigned by VA**, not selected by the lender, borrower, or agent
- The lender may not communicate with the appraiser about value during the appraisal
  process — only administrative matters (scheduling, access, property data)
- Attempting to influence the appraiser's value determination is a federal offense under
  18 U.S.C. § 1014
- The lender may provide factual property information (HOA fees, legal description,
  known repairs) but not suggested value targets or comparables before Tidewater is
  triggered

---

## 6. Appraisal Portability

The VA appraisal (NOV) belongs to the borrower, not the lender. A borrower who receives
a NOV through one lender may:

- Transfer the NOV to a new lender without ordering a new appraisal (within 180-day
  validity)
- Use the NOV at any VA-approved lender for any eligible VA loan product
- Not be charged for a new appraisal by the new lender while the original NOV is valid

The original lender must release the case number (WebLGY case transfer) to the new
lender. The VA may charge a case transfer fee.

---

## 7. Appraisal Timelines

Typical timelines vary by market:

| Market Condition | Inspection Scheduling | Report Delivery | Total Typical Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard markets | 3–7 business days | 5–10 business days | 10–14 business days |
| High-volume markets | 7–14 business days | 7–14 business days | 14–21 business days |
| Rural/remote properties | 14–21 business days | 7–14 business days | 21–35 business days |

Lenders should order the appraisal as early in the contract period as possible,
particularly in rural markets where fee panel appraisers may be scarce.

---

## 8. Lender Overlays on VA Appraisals

Some lenders impose internal standards above VA minimums:

- **Minimum remaining economic life**: Some lenders require more than the VA's 2-year
  roof standard (e.g., 5 years remaining)
- **Condo project re-approval**: Lenders may require a current condo project review even
  if the project is on the VA-approved list
- **Appraisal age at closing**: Some lenders require the appraisal be no older than
  120 days at closing (stricter than the 180-day NOV validity)

These overlays are lender-specific and not VA requirements. Borrowers who encounter an
overlay refusal may shop to another VA-approved lender who does not impose the same
restriction.
