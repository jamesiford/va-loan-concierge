# VA Loan Funding Fee Tables — 2024/2025 Schedule

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 8 | VA Circular 26-24-06 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA fee schedules at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. Overview

The VA funding fee is a one-time charge paid at closing that helps sustain the VA loan
program. It is not a mortgage insurance premium — it is a single payment. Most borrowers
may finance the fee by rolling it into the loan balance rather than paying it in cash at
closing. The fee varies based on:

- **Loan type** (purchase, IRRRL, cash-out refinance, construction, manufactured home)
- **Down payment amount** (purchase loans only)
- **First use vs. subsequent use** of the VA home loan benefit
- **Service-connected disability status**

---

## 2. Purchase Loan Funding Fee Table

### Active Duty / Veterans / National Guard & Reserves (same rate since 2020)

| Down Payment | First Use | Subsequent Use |
|---|---|---|
| Less than 5% | 2.15% | 3.30% |
| 5% or more | 1.50% | 1.50% |
| 10% or more | 1.25% | 1.25% |

> **Note**: Prior to the Blue Water Navy Vietnam Veterans Act (2020), National Guard and
> Reserve members paid a higher rate. Since January 1, 2020, all eligible borrowers pay
> the same rate regardless of service component.

### How Down Payment Tiers Work

The down payment percentage is calculated against the **lesser of the purchase price or
appraised value**. A borrower contributing $20,000 on a $380,000 home (5.26%) qualifies
for the 5% tier. The lender must verify the source of the down payment funds per standard
VA underwriting guidelines.

---

## 3. IRRRL (Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan) Funding Fee

| Loan Type | Fee |
|---|---|
| IRRRL (all borrowers, all uses) | **0.50%** |

The IRRRL funding fee is flat regardless of down payment or whether it is first or
subsequent use. The fee applies to the new loan balance. If the Veteran is rolling closing
costs into the loan, the fee is calculated on the full new balance after adding those costs.

---

## 4. Cash-Out Refinance Funding Fee

| Use | Fee |
|---|---|
| First use | 2.15% |
| Subsequent use | 3.30% |

Cash-out refinance funding fees mirror the purchase loan fee structure (minus down payment
tiers, since there is no down payment on a refinance). A Veteran refinancing a
non-VA loan into a VA loan for the first time pays the first-use rate.

---

## 5. Other Loan Types

| Loan Type | Fee |
|---|---|
| Construction loan | 2.15% (first use) / 3.30% (subsequent) |
| Manufactured home loan (not permanently affixed) | 1.00% |
| Loan assumption | 0.50% |
| Vendee loan (VA-acquired property) | 2.25% |

## 6. Funding Fee Exemptions

The following borrowers are **completely exempt** from the funding fee:

- Veterans receiving VA compensation for a **service-connected disability** (any rating)
- Veterans who would be entitled to receive compensation but for receipt of retirement pay
- Surviving spouses of Veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability
- Veterans rated as having a pre-discharge disability by DoD (disability exam completed
  before discharge)
- Active duty servicemembers who have received the **Purple Heart** (as of January 2020)
- Surviving spouses receiving Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC)

> **Key lender note**: The lender must verify exemption status via the Certificate of
> Eligibility (COE). The COE will indicate "EXEMPT" in the funding fee field if the
> borrower qualifies. Do not collect the fee from an exempt borrower even if the COE
> initially appears to show a fee — contact the VA Regional Loan Center if in doubt.

---

## 7. Financing the Funding Fee

Borrowers may finance 100% of the funding fee into the loan balance, even if doing so
causes the loan to exceed the county conforming loan limit. The financed fee does not
require a down payment offset.

**Example:**
- Purchase price: $400,000
- Down payment: $0 (0%)
- Funding fee: 2.15% × $400,000 = $8,600
- Final loan amount: $408,600

The borrower does not need $8,600 in cash — it is added to the note amount and amortized
over the loan term. VA Form 26-0286 (VA Loan Summary Sheet) must reflect the financed fee.

---

## 8. Subsequent Use — Definition and Tracking

"Subsequent use" applies any time a Veteran has **previously used** the VA home loan
benefit and still has an active VA loan, OR previously used the benefit and paid it off
but did **not** request full entitlement restoration.

A Veteran who used the VA benefit once, paid off the loan in full, and applied for
entitlement restoration (VA Form 26-1880) is treated as a **first-time user** for funding
fee purposes on the next loan — the subsequent-use rate does not apply.

The lender must verify use status via the COE. The system will display "SUBSEQUENT USE"
on the COE when the higher fee applies.

---

## 9. Disability Rating Changes at Closing

If a Veteran is awaiting a disability rating decision and the rating is awarded (or
increased above 0%) **before** the loan closes, the Veteran is exempt from the funding fee
at closing. If the rating is awarded **after** closing, the Veteran may submit VA Form
26-8937 to request a refund of the funding fee paid. The VA will process the refund
directly.

---

## 10. Funding Fee and Net Tangible Benefit (IRRRL)

For IRRRL transactions, the VA's net tangible benefit test requires that the Veteran
recoup all fees, including the funding fee, within **36 months** of the new loan's
closing date. Lenders must run the recoupment calculation including the 0.50% IRRRL fee
in the total costs. If the break-even exceeds 36 months, the IRRRL does not pass the NTB
test regardless of the rate reduction.
