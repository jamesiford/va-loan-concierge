# VA Jumbo Loans, High-Balance Guidelines & Renovation Financing

> **Source**: VA Lender's Handbook Chapter 3 | VA Circular 26-22-08 | Last reviewed: March 2026
> **Disclaimer**: This document contains simulated content for demonstration purposes.
> Always verify current VA jumbo and renovation guidelines at VA.gov or with a VA-approved lender.

---

## 1. VA Jumbo Loans — Overview

The VA does not use the term "jumbo" in its official guidelines — there is no formal
VA jumbo program. However, lenders use the term to describe VA loans **above the
conforming loan limit** set annually by FHFA. Since 2020 (Blue Water Navy Vietnam
Veterans Act), Veterans with **full entitlement** face no effective VA loan limit —
they may borrow as much as a lender will approve, subject to standard credit and income
underwriting.

The "jumbo" designation applies when:
- The loan exceeds the conforming limit ($806,500 in most counties for 2025)
- AND the Veteran does not have full entitlement (has an active VA loan or reduced
  entitlement remaining)

---

## 2. Conforming Loan Limits and High-Cost Counties (2025)

FHFA updates conforming loan limits annually each November for the following year.

| Area Type | 2025 Standard Limit |
|---|---|
| Most U.S. counties | $806,500 |
| High-cost counties (e.g., SF Bay Area, NYC metro, Honolulu) | Up to $1,209,750 |
| Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, USVI | $1,209,750 (statutory maximum) |

For Veterans with full entitlement, these limits are irrelevant — there is no VA loan
cap. For Veterans with reduced entitlement, the guaranty calculation uses the county
limit.

## 3. Down Payment Requirement for High-Balance Loans (Reduced Entitlement)

When a Veteran has reduced entitlement and wishes to purchase a home above the county
conforming limit, a down payment is required. The formula:

```
Required Down Payment = 25% × (Loan Amount − Remaining Entitlement × 4)
```

**Example:**
- Veteran has $80,000 remaining entitlement (active VA loan on another property)
- Desired loan amount: $900,000
- County conforming limit: $806,500
- Remaining entitlement × 4 = $320,000
- Down payment required = 25% × ($900,000 − $320,000) = **$145,000**

The Veteran must have these funds available at closing; they cannot be gifted or borrowed.

## 4. Lender Overlays for High-Balance VA Loans

While the VA has no hard loan limit for Veterans with full entitlement, lenders impose
their own maximum loan amounts above which they will not lend VA loans. Common lender
overlays:

| Loan Amount | Typical Overlay Requirements |
|---|---|
| $806,500–$1,000,000 | No overlay at many lenders; standard VA credit underwriting |
| $1,000,000–$1,500,000 | Many lenders require minimum 720 FICO; 12 months reserves |
| $1,500,000–$2,000,000 | Most lenders require 740+ FICO; 12–24 months PITI reserves |
| Above $2,000,000 | Very few VA lenders; typically require full documentation + reserves |

These overlays are lender policy, not VA requirements. Veterans who are declined by one
lender for an above-limit VA loan may find approval at a specialty VA lender.

## 5. Secondary Market Treatment of High-Balance VA Loans

Ginnie Mae securitizes VA loans through the following MBS pools:

- **Ginnie Mae I (single-issuer)**: Single-family loans; maximum loan size aligns with
  the conventional conforming limit
- **Ginnie Mae II (multi-issuer)**: Accommodates higher balance loans; preferred pool
  for VA jumbo loans
- **VA Jumbo pools**: Specialty pools for loans above standard conforming limits; higher
  guaranty fee pricing applies for the issuer

Lenders originating high-balance VA loans must ensure their warehouse line and
securitization conduit can accommodate the loan size. VA loans above $2M may need to
be held in portfolio by the originating lender.

## 6. VA Renovation Financing

The VA does not offer a native renovation loan equivalent to FHA's 203(k) program.
However, Veterans have several paths to finance home improvements:

### 6.1 Cash-Out Refinance (Most Common)
- Refinance the existing VA loan (or any mortgage) into a new VA loan
- Take cash out up to 100% of the appraised value of the improved home
- Use cash proceeds for renovations
- **Best for**: Veterans who have built equity and want to finance improvements after
  purchase; existing VA homeowners

### 6.2 Purchase + Simultaneous Renovation (Lender-Specific Overlay)
Some VA-approved lenders offer a **VA renovation loan** product as a lender overlay —
a VA purchase loan structured to include renovation costs. This is not a VA program;
it is the lender's proprietary product using VA guidelines as the base:
- Loan amount = lesser of purchase price + renovation costs OR appraised value after
  improvements ("as-improved" value)
- VA appraisal uses the "as-completed" value
- Renovation funds are held in escrow and disbursed as work is completed
- Lenders typically require a licensed contractor and a construction timeline
- Not all VA lenders offer this product — borrowers should shop specifically for
  "VA renovation loan" lenders

### 6.3 VA Energy Efficient Mortgage (EEM)
- Allows Veterans to finance energy efficiency improvements as part of a VA purchase or
  refinance
- Loan amount may exceed the NOV by up to **$6,000** (or up to $6,000 above the NOV)
  without a new appraisal if the improvements are cost-effective
- Eligible improvements: insulation, solar panels, heat pumps, weather stripping,
  storm windows, programmable thermostats
- Requires an energy audit and documentation of cost-effectiveness

### 6.4 VA Adapted Housing Grants (Not a Loan)
For Veterans with qualifying service-connected disabilities:
- **SAH Grant (Specially Adapted Housing)**: Up to $109,986 (FY2025) for Veterans
  with specific mobility impairments; modifies or builds a home to meet disability needs
- **SHA Grant (Special Housing Adaptation)**: Up to $22,036 (FY2025) for a broader
  set of qualifying disabilities
- These are grants, not loans — no repayment required

## 7. VA Jumbo vs. Conventional Jumbo — Comparison

| Dimension | VA High-Balance | Conventional Jumbo |
|---|---|---|
| Down payment (full entitlement) | $0 | 10–20% typical |
| Down payment (reduced entitlement) | Calculated gap only | Full conventional requirement |
| PMI / Mortgage insurance | None | None (conventional jumbo has no PMI) |
| Funding fee | Yes (2.15% first use) | N/A |
| Credit score (VA minimum) | No official minimum | Lender sets minimum (typically 700–720+) |
| Credit score (lender overlay) | 620–680 typical for standard; 720+ for $1M+ | 700–740 typical |
| Debt-to-income | VA residual income test + 41% DTI guideline | Typically max 43% DTI |
| Interest rate vs. conventional jumbo | Often 0.25–0.50% lower | Baseline |
| Reserves required | None VA-required; lender overlay varies | 12–24 months typical |

For Veterans purchasing in high-cost markets, the VA high-balance loan often provides a
material cost advantage over conventional jumbo financing, particularly through the
elimination of PMI and the lower down payment requirement.
